Firebase Setup
Connect your app to live database
Step 1 — Create Firebase Project
1
Open Firebase Console console.firebase.google.com
↗
2
Click "Add project"Sign in with your Google account
3
Name it anythinge.g. "wok-and-fire" or "my-restaurant"
4
Disable Google AnalyticsNot needed → click "Create project"
Step 1b — Enable Firestore
5
Left sidebar → Build → Firestore DatabaseInside your new project
6
Click "Create database"Choose "Start in test mode" → Next
7
Select your regionasia-south1 (Mumbai) recommended for India
Step 1c — Enable Sign-In Methods
8
Left sidebar → Build → AuthenticationThen click "Get started"
9
Click "Sign-in method" tabYou'll see a list of providers
10
Enable Email/PasswordClick it → toggle ON → Save
11
Enable GoogleClick it → toggle ON → enter your email → Save
12
Authorized domains → Add your Netlify URLe.g. yourapp.netlify.app — required for Google Sign-In to work
Step 2 — Get Your Config
1
Click ⚙️ Project SettingsGear icon next to "Project Overview"
2
Scroll to "Your apps"Under the General tab
3
Click </> Web app iconRegister with any nickname
4
Copy the firebaseConfig objectYou'll see apiKey, projectId, etc.
// This is what Firebase shows you — copy these values
const firebaseConfig = {
  apiKey: "AIzaSy...",
  authDomain: "myapp.firebaseapp.com",
  projectId: "my-project-id",
  storageBucket: "myapp.appspot.com",
  messagingSenderId: "123456789",
  appId: "1:123:web:abc"
};

Fill in each value from your Firebase project settings
